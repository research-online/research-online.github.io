Elisa Cavatorta

I study behaviour and beliefs

About

I am an Associate Professor in Political Economy at King's College London who studies conflict, individual preferences and beliefs, and policy evaluation. I obtained my PhD in Economics from Birkbeck College, University of London and I was a Post-Doctoral Research Fellow at SOAS, University of London, and at the Institute for Fiscal Studies before joining King's College London.

My research uses applied economic theory and applied econometrics to provide policy relevant answers to questions in the fields of development and public policy. I have an interest in the evaluation of policy interventions and the development of new measurement tools for applied behavioural research.

I address questions like: how and why living in a violent context shapes the formation of people's preferences and beliefs? What factors drive the willingness and the ability to negotiate and cooperate? What conditions and interventions facilitate cooperation and improve socio-psychological wellbeing?

I use a variety of methods, lab and field experiments, RCTs, interactive surveys and structural models and more recently qualitative methods.

Beside research and teaching, I am a tireless dancer and have a passion for my piano and the charm of the Middle East.

My research has been funded by the LSE Middle East Centre (2017), the Qatar National Research Foundation (2016), King's ESRC Transformative Research Fellowship (2015), the AXA Research Fund (2013-15), Global Development Network-Economic Research Forum (2012), Georgetown University (2011), SOAS Faculty of Law and Social Sciences (2012) and Birkbeck College School Fund (2012).

Research

My current research has two themes. I study the impact of exposure to political violence on the formation of beliefs, preferences and behaviour towards others, and the design and evaluation of policy interventions to improve wellbeing. I design and evaluate new measurement tools for applied research on individual behaviour.
